Welcome! We're excited you're interested in starting a Civic Tech Project. Depending on the maturity of your idea, you'll join either the 
**Onboarding Track** or the **Incubator Track.**

First, let's check your eligibility.

## Eligibility

We welcome projects from:

- **Nonprofits**: Digitizing paper processes, building tools, or creating proofs-of-concept that support mission-driven work.
- **Community organizations**: Applying similar approaches to create direct community impact.
- **Local government**: Community feedback tools, service request systems, or new ways government can engage constituents.
- **Engaged community members**: If you're close to a problem, you likely know it best. We want to hear your ideas.

Don't fit neatly into one of these? You're still welcome to pitch.

#### What we don't support

- **Electoral work** : Civic Tech DC does not run campaigns or endorse candidates. We are strictly nonpartisan. Advocacy is welcome, but outputs must serve the public interest and be usable across party lines.
- **Startups** : While we are working to expand our *programming* to startups, startups with civic-focused components that meet all project requirements may be eligible. You are welcome to talk to us.

## Becoming a Project

Your project must meet all four criteria:

| | Criteria | Details |
|---|---|---|
| ✔ | **It's civic tech** | The project must relate to civic tech in some meaningful way. *(See [definition](https://civictechdc.netlify.app/whatiscivictech/))* |
| ✔ | **It's open source** | Open source lets volunteers contribute. All projects live on our GitHub *(civictechdc/civictechdc-website)* |
| ✔ | **It benefits a real community or partner** | A clear end user or validator ensures the project addresses an actual need. |
| ✔ | **You're committed as project lead** | All projects are volunteer-run. You (or a teammate) must be available to define scope and guide volunteers. Timelines are flexible and we'll help you scope to fit your availability. |

#### Where you land depends on your answers

 ✅ **Yes to all four**: You're ready for the *Onboarding Track!*

 ❌ **No to civic tech or open source**: Unfortunately, we can't accept the project. However, as we share above, the definition of what counts 
as civic tech is very broad and you are welcome to talk to us.

 ❌ **No to community benefit**: You'll start in the *Incubator Track* while we help develop that piece.

 ❌ **No to project lead**: No problem! You can still pitch your idea and participate as an Advisor.
